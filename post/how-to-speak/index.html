Motivation #

The quality of communication is determined by: $Q_{c} = \int (K, P, t)$.

$K$ is how much knowledge you have, $P$ is how much you practice on your knowledge and $t$ is your inherent talent. Note that $t$ is rather small, what does matter is what you know.

For example, if you are skilled at skiing, you must do a better job in a ski resort than a Olympic vault champion, because you have the knowledge and practice while he only have the talent.

How to Start #

  1. DO NOT START WITH A JOKE.

  2. Start with a promise such as “you will know after 60 minutes” is a better idea.

  3. Cycle on the subject to emphasize what you want to convey.

  4. Build a fence not to confuse people what you are talking.

  5. Use verbal punctuation, to let people know where they are.

  6. Ask a question and pause about 7 seconds.

Time and Place #

  1. In MIT, tale at 11 a.m. because students have waken up.

  2. The light should be strong not to let people fall asleep.

  3. Go to your presenting venue if possible.

  4. Seats do not have to be occupied entirely, but it is better being half occupied.

Boards, Props and Slides #

  1. Teach with a board and present with your slides.

  2. Board can allow you to use graphics and the writing speed is fairly appropriate for people to digest.

  3. Use props if you can.

  4. Slides tips:

    • Do not read

    • Be close to you slides

    • Image must be simple

    • Title does not have to be presented on the slides

    • Face to your audience.

    • Do not use pointer tool, use a arrow on you slides

    • Do not make you slide heavy, allow blank

    • Present some fancy demos

Persuasion #

JOB TALKS

  • Say you VISION and what you have DONE SOMETHING in 5 minutes.

  • List your contributions in the final.

Your ideas are your children, don’t make them go into the world in rags.

Winston Star

  • symbol associated with your work

  • slogan

  • surprise

  • salient ideas

  • tell the story

How to End #

  • Final slide: List your contributions

  • Final words can be : a joke, a blessing, a salution
